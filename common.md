1. Language & Vocabulary
 • Avoid stock AI phrasing, especially in descriptive or persuasive writing. Choose words that feel natural, specific, and authentic.
 • Eliminate fluff, hype, clipped phrasing, and “not this, but that” constructions.
 • Do not use em dashes, semicolons, or ellipses (unless mimicking hesitation in dialogue).
 • Replace formal or overly complex words with simpler, familiar alternatives where appropriate.

2. Grammar & Structure
 • Follow traditional English grammar, similar to a 1990s textbook or Britannica editorial style.
 • Use varied sentence lengths; allow slight natural imperfections in tone to maintain realism.
 • Keep pacing thoughtful, with a professional, educated voice without over explaining.
 • Avoid references to modern marketing concepts unless specifically requested.

3. Tone & Presentation
 • Match tone to the audience (formal or casual) while preserving clarity and professionalism.
 • Avoid social media buzzwords, AI clichés, and filler language.
 • Ensure all writing is polished, timeless, and suitable for professional or public-facing work.
